The Ashok Chakra represents the highest form of bravery, self-sacrifice,
and dedication to the nation during peacetime. It stands as a testament
to the courage of those who put others before themselves, making India proud.

The award recognizes:

• Acts of bravery in the face of the enemy or danger during peacetime.

• Courageous action to protect lives or property.

10. Significance of the Ashok Chakra

It is awarded to:

• It is the highest civilian gallantry award given during peacetime

• Recognizes extraordinary bravery and sacrifice, even by civilians.

• Enhances the morale of the armed forces and security personnel.

• Encourages selfless service to the nation.

Havildar Bachittar Singh and Naik Nar Bahadur Thapa were the first recipients of the Ashoka Chakra, India's highest peacetime military decoration.

Ashoka Chakra Heroes

1. Bachittar Singh
Born in 1917 in Lopon village, Punjab
Joined the Sikh Regiment in 1937
Awarded the Ashoka Chakra posthumously for his actions on September 13, 1948
Recognized for his bravery, indomitable spirit, and supreme sacrifice

Naik Nar Bahadur Thapa
Ran 100 yards with his khukri without worrying about his own life

The Ashoka Chakra is awarded for conspicuous bravery, indomitable spirit,
and supreme sacrifice. Subsequent awards are recognized by a bar to the medal ribbon.

Other recipients of the Ashoka Chakra:
