1. Introduction

The Ashok Chakra is India's highest peacetime military decoration,
awarded for valor, courageous action, or self-sacrifice away from the battlefield.
It is equivalent to the Param Vir Chakra, which is awarded during wartime


2. History and Establishment

• The Ashok Chakra was established on 4 January 1952 by the Government of India.

• It was initially known as the Ashoka Chakr

Ashoka Chakra, Class I (renamed Ashok Chakra in 1967).
Ashoka Chakra, Class II (renamed Kirti Chakra in 1967).
Ashoka Chakra, Class III (renamed Shaurya Chakra in 1967



3. Purpose

It is awarded to:

• Military personnel

• Civilians

• Police officers

• Paramilitary forces

The award recognizes:

• Acts of bravery in the face of the enemy or danger during peacetime.

• Courageous action to protect lives or property.




4. Design of the Medal

• The medal is circular, made of gold-gilded bronze, and is 1.375 inches in diameter.

• The reverse side is plain with the words "Ashok Chakra" in both Hindi and English, separated by lotus flowers.

• It is worn on the left chest, suspended by a green ribbon with an orange stripe in the middle.
